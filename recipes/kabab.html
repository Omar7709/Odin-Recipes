Seekh Kabab

Description

Seekh Kabab is a famous barbecue recipe originating from central asia. It goes by many names from culture to culture. In Turkey and Arabia it is popularly known as Seesh Kebab. It is prepared by mincing mutton or beef and adding a blend of spices to add flavor. The meat is then rolled against skewers to form long tubes. These skewers are slow cooked on coal which gives it a brown color and smoky flavour. the Seekh kabab is eaten with freshly baked round bread called naan which is prepared in the tandoor oven. This dish is enjoyed in outdoor gatherings and parties. Seekh Kabab also famous to be a staple during marriage feasts and birthday dinners.

Ingredients

  • 500g Minced meat (can be chicken, meat or beef)
  • 2 Tablespoon salt
  • 2 Red Chillies
  • 2 Green Chillies
  • 2 tablespoon ginger garlic paste
  • Lemon to taste

Steps

For preparing fire

Make sure to pre-heat the grill by adding food grade charcoal and set them afire. Remember to gauge fire temperature so that the meat may not be burnt or overcooked.

For kebabs

  1. Chop all the chillies and add them to the meat
  2. Add in salt and ginger-garlic paste
  3. Add freshly squeezed lemon juice
  4. Thoroughly mix all of the ingredients together
  5. Squeeze and roll this mixture against the skewers to form long tube-like figures. You can use as many skewers as you like
  6. Place the skewers on charcoal, slowly turning them as the meat begins to cook
  7. When the meat is light brownish, take off the skewers. Serve with bread or rice
